Actividad Física [Sitio Externo]

Actividad Física

La actividad física regular es especialmente importante para las personas con enfermedades como diabetes, presión arterial alta o colesterol alto. El objetivo es mantenerse activo y PERMANECER haciendo actividades agradables y, por lo tanto, sostenibles. Hacer ejercicio con regularidad puede reducir la glucosa en sangre, la presión arterial y los niveles de colesterol, e incluso reducir el estrés. Un aumento en la actividad liviana aumenta el estado físico, así que inquiétese, juegue y haga las tareas del hogar. Haga que caminar sea un hábito diario, para sus viajes al trabajo, diligencias, escaleras y ejercicios, y aumente el número de pasos

Sueño [Sitio Externo]

Sueño

La cantidad de horas de sueño que un paciente duerme es un factor importante en su bienestar general. Los adultos sanos necesitan de 7 a 8 horas de sueño al día para un desempeño óptimo, y los niños y adolescentes necesitan más. El sueño inadecuado prolongado puede causar presión arterial alta, ataque cardíaco, obesidad, accidente cerebrovascular, depresión, deterioro mental y, en general, una mala calidad de vida. Mantener un horario de sueño constante ayuda a mantener los ciclos regulares y promueve un sueño de mejor calidad. Cree un ritual relajante a la hora de acostarse y mantenga las computadoras portátiles fuera del dormitorio. Las camas son para el sexo y el descanso. Reconocer los problemas con los comportamientos del sueño y delinear los pasos para mejorar son una parte importante del plan de atención.

Hábitos [Sitio Externo]

Hábitos

Es importante identificar y reconocer el impacto de los malos hábitos como fumar, el consumo excesivo de alcohol, el uso de sustancias e incluso el consumo de alimentos nocivos. Los planes de atención pueden delinear los pasos para detener estos comportamientos que pueden conducir a una variedad de condiciones de salud deficientes.

Nutrition

A nutrition plan must recognize the current problems in eating habits and consider chronic conditions/symptoms, food preparation capabilities, and dietary restrictions of the individual. The outer aisles of the supermarket are where the healthiest foods are: greens, fruits, poultry, nuts, grains, and dairy. Fill up the cart.
Meals at home and a brownbag lunch at work are more nutritious than eating out. Families who eat the evening meal together eat healthier.9

View more at carecards.me

Physical Activity

Regular physical activity is especially important for those with conditions like diabetes, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol. The goal is to get active and STAY doing enjoyable and, therefore, sustainable activities. Exercising regularly can lower your blood glucose, blood pressure, and cholesterol levels, and even reduce stress. An increase in light activity increases fitness, so fidget and putter and do housework. Make walking a daily habit — for your commute, errands, stairs, and fitness — and get that step count going up.9

View more at carecards.me

Sleep

The amount of sleep a patient gets is a significant factor on their overall wellbeing. Healthy adults need 7-8 hours of sleep a day for optimal performance, and children and teens need more. Prolonged inadequate sleep can cause high blood pressure, heart attack, obesity, stroke, depression, mental impairment, and generally poor quality of life. Maintaining a consistent sleep schedule helps keep your cycles regular and promotes better quality sleep. Create a relaxing bedtime ritual and keep laptops out of the bedroom. Beds are for sex and rest. Recognizing problems with sleep behaviors and outlining the steps to improvement are an important part of the care plan.9

View more at carecards.me

Mental Resilience

Management of stress and any mental, cognitive, or mood symptoms is another, very complex part of the care plan. Prolonged exposure to stress can be unhealthy, and cause or make it difficult to manage conditions like diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and psychiatric illnesses. Chronic stress can negatively impact on your immune, digestive, renal (kidneys), and reproductive systems. It is essential to take the proper steps eliminate or cope with stressors in an effective way, as well as manage any symptoms of mental illness.9

View more at carecards.me

Medication Management

Recognizing a need for change in the medication regimen allows for the creation of a treatment plan. A vital part of incorporating this into the care plan is ensuring the adherence to the prescribed medication. Patients who stick to their medications have better outcomes. Quitting early means health suffers.9
It is important to ask and understand why a medication is necessary, how to properly take it, what side effects are causes for alarm, and who to talk to if issues arise.9

View more at carecards.me

Prioritize Sexual Health

Good health bolsters your sex life; your sex life bolsters your health. Regular sex — once a week or more — is associated with greater immunity, less stress, fewer heart attacks, longer life, and unleashes mood-boosting endorphins. If worries or physical challenges are hindering your enjoyment of sex, talk to your partner and possibly a doctor for reassurance and advice. To avoid HIV infection and sexually transmitted diseases (STDs), a condom is the first line of defense. It forms a barrier between the penis and anus, vagina, or mouth. Bodily fluids cannot get past it. For safer oral sex, there are dental dams.9

View more at carecards.me

Kick Bad Habits

It’s important to identify and recognize the impact of bad habits such as smoking, alcohol over consumption, substance use, and even consumption of harmful foods. Care plans can outline the steps to stop these behaviors that can lead to a variety of poor health conditions.9

View more at carecards.me

Know Your Numbers

A few minutes of daily head-to-toe visual checks helps you notice changes, like a new freckle or unusual hair growth or loss. Monthly checks of skin and your male or female parts give you information to share with your doctor. Especially relevant to patients managing chronic conditions, monitoring of relevant vitals (such as weight, blood pressure, daily steps, blood sugar, etc.) is an essential part of a care plan and self care.9

View more at carecards.me

Manage Your Daily Activities

Any other problems that affect the patient’s general wellbeing can be relevant to the care plan. These can be things such as personal hygiene, functioning around the house, management of finances, transportation, safety behaviors, etc.9

View more at carecards.me

How Care Plans Work

A care plan applies the different areas of concern (i.e. physical activity, nutrition, etc.) across the universal components (i.e. goals, instructions, etc.). There is a baseline care plan that exists for general healthy living, and this baseline becomes more customized to a person as more information is collected. Factors such as sex, age, ethnicity, activities, conditions, etc. influence what content goes in a care plan, and how important those areas are to the person.

Improve posture to reduce neck pain and stiffness.

Review quality of neck posture in 6 months, refer to Orthopedic if no improvement.

Review dental health in 6 months.
